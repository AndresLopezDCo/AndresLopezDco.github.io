Mental Health Project - BC

In a mission to enhance healthcare resource distribution, I conducted a comprehensive analysis of occupancy rates in various healthcare facilities. My findings provided actionable insights that can significantly impact patient care and resource allocation in the healthcare sector.

The Mental Health and Substance Use (MSHU) Bed database analysis has provided valuable insights into the distribution of beds, population, category of healthcare, and the number of facilities in British Columbia. By analyzing the data from the MSHU across local health areas, authority areas, and the province. This analysis provides a detailed analysis useful for healthcare providers, and other stakeholders to develop strategies to improve access to mental health and substance use services for all British Columbians.

PrioritizationMatrix

In the dynamic oil and gas industry, maintaining the structural integrity of mechanical assets, especially pipelines, is of utmost importance. This project involves the use of data from instrumented inspections as a cost-effective means to pinpoint metal loss areas and potential leaks.

Classification Model

Metal loss features were labeled according to their dimensions.

Comparison field - tool

Comparison between the true value (field) and the one detected by the instrumented tool.

Comparison field - prediction

Comparison between the true value (field) and the predicted base on the measurement by the tool.

Analysis of the systems 1

Considered the mean error of all the comparisons between field and tool measures for the system.

Analysis of the systems 2

Considered the mean error of all the comparisons between field and predicted measures for the system..

Analysis of non-paired measurements

Findings in the field not reported by the tool.
